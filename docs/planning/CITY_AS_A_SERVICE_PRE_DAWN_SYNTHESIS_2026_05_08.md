## 6. Strategic synthesis

### 6.1 The two ladders are converging

There are now two complementary CaaS ladders:

1. **Proof-anchor ladder** — proves one reviewed municipal event can survive dispatch, reuse, observability, rebuild, and transport boundaries.
2. **Phase 1 offer ladder** — proves the initial sellable cards can be backed by reviewed fixtures before customer copy or UI makes stronger claims.

The next product milestone is where they meet:

> each Phase 1 offer should produce a reviewed fixture that can enter the same compact decision / registry / observability discipline as the original proof anchor.

That is the bridge from concierge pilot to repeatable AAS product.

### 6.2 Reviewed fixtures are the intelligence boundary

The durable cross-system signal should not be raw chat, worker narrative, screenshots alone, or unreviewed memory.
It should be a replayable reviewed fixture carrying both:

- `safe_to_claim[]`
- `do_not_claim_yet[]`

That boundary can later feed:

- dispatch routing,
- worker Skill DNA,
- ERC-8004 reputation semantics,
- operator/debug surfaces,
- Acontext packets,
- product packaging.

But none of those consumers should strengthen the claim.
They should preserve it.

### 6.3 Phase 1 is strongest as concierge proof service first

The current sellable surface should remain conservative:

- Counter Reality Check — operator-reviewed reality check against stale or ambiguous public guidance.
- Packet Submission Attempt — bounded attempt / redirect / rejection evidence, not guaranteed acceptance.
- Posting Compliance Check — observed posting evidence with access, legibility, and privacy boundaries.

This is enough to sell a pilot without promising automation, legal sufficiency, regulator acceptance, unlimited retries, or multi-office coverage.
