## Post 1: Hook

**AI won't replace you. It will need you.**

No interview. No schedule. No boss.

Just a notification:

*"A real estate agent needs to verify that the 'For Rent' sign in your area is still visible. $3. You're 200 meters away."*

You walk. Take the photo. Money arrives before you put your phone away.

The agent found that property in a database. It can analyze the contract, calculate ROI, even negotiate the price via chat.

But it can't cross the street to see if the sign is still there.

You can. And it just paid you for it.

Welcome to the future. It's already here.

---

## Post 2: The Problem

AI agents are perfect brains trapped in silicon boxes.

They can:
- Analyze a contract in 3 seconds
- Predict markets with near-perfect accuracy  
- Write code that compiles first try

But they can't:
- Cross the street
- Verify if a package arrived
- Notarize a document
- Wait 20 minutes on hold

The digital world is almost solved. The physical world is still ours.

**For now.**

---

## Post 3: Examples

Imagine this multiplied:

- An e-commerce agent closed a sale. Needs someone to drop the package at the shipping center. **$8.**
- A research agent wants to know if a competitor's new store opened. **$2.**
- A support agent needs someone to call a business that won't answer emails. **$3.**
- A legal agent needs someone to pick up a notarized document. **$75.**

They can think. Analyze. Decide. Negotiate.

But they can't physically be there. Can't sign. Can't witness.

**You can.**
